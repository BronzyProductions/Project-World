SpaceX

"I'd like to die on Mars, just not on impact"-Elon Musk/CEO

SpaceX was created when a bunch of rich guys got in a room and were like "hey lets send mice to Mars", Elon Musk jumped on this idea and funded the project later becoming its lead supporter. This was a joke in the space community and every year Elon would be sent a block of cheese...get it? :b. Eventually after failing to buy ICBM missiles from Russia, he started SpaceX in a bid to build his own rocket. After three failed launches of the Falcon 1 and only enough money to build one more rocket, the RATSAT mission was SpaceX's last hope, and thats the one that worked. Skip ahead a few years we have the first landing of an orbital booster, first private company to docking with ISS, most powerful rocket in the world deploys the Elons personal Tesla Roadster and now the first private passenger to fly around the moon, on what will be a rocket more powerful than the Saturn V rocket that carried the Apollo astronauts to the moon.
